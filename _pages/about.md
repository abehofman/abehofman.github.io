I am an Assistant Professor of Psychological Methods at the [University of Amsterdam](https://www.uva.nl/). My research sits at the intersection of **psychometrics** and **educational data science**, with a focus on large-scale adaptive learning systems.

I am the founder and PI of [EdAptiv.org](https://www.EdAptiv.org), an interuniversity alliance connecting edtech researchers across universities — open to collaborations with academic and educational partners. I also work closely with [Prowise Learn](https://www.prowise.com/learn/), one of the Netherlands' largest adaptive learning platforms, where millions of children practice mathematics, spelling, and reading daily.

## Research Interests

My work centers on understanding how children learn in digital environments, and how we can use that data to improve both research and practice:

- **Psychometrics of adaptive learning** — Elo-based ability tracking, item response theory, measuring learning at scale
- **Motivation and engagement** — individual differences in quitting, error processing, and persistence in online learning
- **Educational data mining** — extracting insights from large behavioral logs
- **Cognitive development** — skill acquisition, working memory, and mathematical development in children

## EdAptiv

[EdAptiv.org](https://www.EdAptiv.org) is an open interuniversity research alliance that brings together researchers working on adaptive learning technology. We combine rich behavioral data from learning platforms with psychometric and cognitive models to study how children learn — and how systems can be designed to support them better. We welcome collaborations from both academic and educational partners.
